
### 4. FAQ SECTION (200-300 words)

**Include 3-5 questions related to your topic:**

```markdown
## Frequently Asked Questions (H2)

### What makes a good dating profile? (H3)
A good dating profile is authentic, specific, and showcases your personality. 
It should include clear photos, a compelling bio that highlights your interests, 
and honest information about what you're looking for. Avoid clichés and be 
genuine—people can tell when you're being real.

### How long should my dating bio be? (H3)
Aim for 150-300 words. Long enough to give people a sense of who you are, 
but short enough to keep them interested. Focus on quality over quantity—
every sentence should add value.

### Should I mention what I'm looking for in a relationship? (H3)
Yes! Being upfront about your relationship goals helps attract compatible 
matches. If you're seeking a serious relationship, say so. This filters out 
people looking for casual dating and saves everyone time.

### How often should I update my profile? (H3)
Update your profile every 2-3 months or whenever something significant 
changes in your life. Fresh content keeps your profile active in the 
algorithm and shows you're an engaged user.

### What photos should I avoid on dating apps? (H3)
Avoid group photos where you're hard to identify, heavily filtered images, 
sunglasses in every photo, gym selfies, and photos with ex-partners. Choose 
clear, recent photos that show your face and personality.
```

**FAQ Best Practices:**
- Answer real questions users ask
- Use question format in H3 headings
- Provide detailed, helpful answers
- Include target keyword in 1-2 answers
- Add FAQSchema markup for rich snippets

## CONTENT QUALITY GUIDELINES

### Writing Style
- **Tone:** Friendly, supportive, expert but approachable
- **Voice:** Second person ("you") for engagement
- **Perspective:** Empathetic and understanding
- **Language:** Simple, clear, conversational
- **Reading Level:** 8th-grade (use Hemingway Editor)

### Formatting
- **Paragraphs:** 2-4 sentences max
- **Sentences:** 15-20 words average
- **Lists:** Use bullet points and numbered lists
- **Bold:** Highlight key points
- **Italics:** Use sparingly for emphasis
- **Quotes:** Include expert quotes or testimonials

### Content Elements
- **Statistics:** Include relevant data and studies
- **Examples:** Provide real-world examples
- **Actionable Tips:** Give specific, implementable advice
- **Visuals:** Add images, infographics, or screenshots
- **Comparisons:** Use before/after or good/bad examples

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### How long should a blog post be?
Aim for 1500-2500 words. Longer content tends to rank better, but quality 
matters more than quantity. Focus on comprehensively answering the user's 
question.

### How many keywords should I target per post?
Focus on one primary keyword and 3-5 related keywords. Don't try to rank 
for too many keywords in a single post.

### How often should I publish new posts?
Consistency matters more than frequency. Aim for 1-2 high-quality posts per 
week rather than daily low-quality posts.

### Should I update old blog posts?
Yes! Update posts every 6-12 months with fresh information, new examples, 
and current statistics. This signals to Google that your content is current.

### How do I know if my post is working?
Monitor these metrics in Google Search Console:
- Impressions (how often it appears in search)
- Clicks (how often people click)
- Average position (where it ranks)
- CTR (click-through rate)
